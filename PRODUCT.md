# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo engineers and sysadmins who manage multiple servers daily. They work in focused, keyboard-driven sessions, often context-switching between machines. They care about speed and reliability above all — connecting to the wrong server, or waiting on a sluggish UI, has real-world costs. They are technical enough to have opinions about their tools and will notice craft details.

## Product Purpose

Naden is a fast, credential-safe desktop client for engineers who manage many servers. It replaces scattered `~/.ssh/config` files and terminal history with an organized, searchable inventory, a built-in multi-tab terminal, SFTP access, and an AES-256 vault for credentials — all without ever syncing secrets remotely. Success looks like: open the app, connect to the right server in under five seconds, never worry about credential security.

## Brand Personality

Powerful, polished, confident. The tool that respects your workflow — not a product trying to look like a product. Feels premium without being precious. Distinctive enough that users want to show it off, restrained enough that it disappears when you're working.

## Anti-references

- **TablePlus / Sequel Pro** — heavy macOS-native chrome, traditional toolbar aesthetic, legacy database-tool feel
- **Hyper / retro terminal** — green-on-black hacker aesthetic, retro glow, nostalgia as design direction
- **Generic VS Code extensions** — bland sidebar + list, no visual character, looks assembled not designed
- **SaaS dashboards (Vercel, Linear)** — overpolished startup UI, metric card grids, gradient hero sections, "growth" aesthetic

## Design Principles

1. **Speed as respect** — every interaction should feel instant; latency and loading states signal disrespect for the user's time. Motion that communicates, never delays.
2. **Confidence through clarity** — unambiguous state at a glance. Connected, loading, failed, locked — never a mystery. Information hierarchy earns trust.
3. **Power without ceremony** — advanced features (jump hosts, bulk actions, audit log) are reachable without polluting the primary workflow. Depth hides behind affordances, not buried in settings.
4. **Tool, not toy** — beauty comes from precision and restraint, not decoration. Every visual decision must pull weight. If removing it doesn't hurt, it shouldn't be there.
5. **Identity without cliché** — looks unmistakably like Naden, not "a developer tool in dark mode." The neon-on-near-black palette is a committed choice, not a default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text). Reduced motion respected throughout (`prefers-reduced-motion`). Keyboard-first: every action reachable without a mouse. Focus indicators present and visible. Color is never the sole indicator of state.
